Book recomendations

Causal inference books

  • Causal Inference for Statistics, Social, and Biomedical Sciences: An Introduction by Imbens and Rubin
  • Impact Evaluation in Practice free pdf
  • Causality: Models, Reasoning and Inference by Judea Pearl.

    Pearl’s book really doesn’t do a good job on inference and requires a complete language change to understand things that were already well understood in the social sciences for years. I would put it down the list and read Imbens and Rubin first; then later, look at Pearl and the Pearl/Heckman controversy. I would argue that Pearl’s notion of causality is comparable to that of Wold and Strotz in economics in the 1950’s anf 60’s, so called triangular or recursive models. Interesting stuff, but limiting, as so much more is possible with the more general models we all use now.

  • Causal inference in economics and marketing, a nice intro paper for people with a machine learning background
  • Consumer Heterogeneity and Paid Search Effectiveness: A Large Scale Field Experiment, a paper that " mostly demonstrates the problems with using observational data to infer causal impacts, especially in the context of paid search internet marketing (your backyard) where endogeneity is very serious. (“endogeneity” = the economists’ term for “correlation between the unmeasured error term and some independent variables”, which causes a regression coefficient to be biased.)"
